Mountain Chicken
Habitat: Cloud forests of the Caribbean
Diet: Insects, fruits, and leaves
About: The Mountain Chicken is a large, flightless bird native to the Caribbean. It is known for its distinctive call and is critically endangered due to habitat loss and hunting.
Care & Wellness: Provide a spacious aviary with plenty of perches and hiding spots. A diet rich in fruits and insects is essential for their health.
Behavior: Mountain Chickens are known for their loud calls and are most active at night. They are social birds and often found in pairs or small groups.
Conservation Status: Critically Endangered
